1. Introduction¶

The Titanic passenger data was analyzed for this project. The questions explored are:

  1. What was the difference in survival between male and female passengers?
  2. How did survival vary between passenger classes?
  3. What is the correlation between age and survival?
  4. Did survival between passengers from different ports of embarkation vary?

2. Data import, check data types and limitations¶

The data was first imported from the provided Titanic csv file. The data types for some of the columns did not match the data. For example, 'PassengerId' and 'Pclass' were imported as integers. However, this will not affect the analysis as calculations will not be perfomed on these values.

In addition, 20 % of the age data was missing from the data. This is a limitation on the analysis of age and survival which will be shown later. The pandas functions used in the analysis (groupby, mean, sum and count) ignore missing (NaN) values in grouping and calculations.

3. What was the difference in survival between male and female passengers?¶

There was a difference in the survival rate between male and female passengers. 74% of female passengers survived while 19% of male passengers survived. The plot below shows the number of passengers that survived for each gender and the total number of passengers.

4. How did survival vary between passenger classes?¶

The survival numbers across the three different passenger classes were explored. Due to the discrepancy in survival between male and female passengers, the data for each class was explored by gender.

The survival rate between male and female passengers in each class was plotted as shown below. This showed that more than 90% of female passengers in the first and second class survived, while about 50% of female passengers in the third class survived. The survival rate for male passengers was lower. About 35% of first class male passengers survived and about 15% of second and third class male passengers survived.

5. What is the correlation between age and survival?¶

In order to examine any correlation between age and survival, the number of passengers per age group was first plotted as shown below. The ages were divided into groups every 10 years starting at age 0. The average age was 29.7 years with a standard deviation of 14.5 years. The median age was 28.0 years. The age distribution was fairly normal as reflected in the close mean and median values and the age distribution plot below.

Next, the survival rate per age group was calculated and plotted as shown below. Passengers less than 10 years old had the highest survival rate of 61.3%. The survival rate for passenger from 10 - 70 years old had survival rates from 35-45%. The survival rate for passengers from 70-80 years old was 0%.

One limitation of this analysis is that there were only 19 records for passengers from 60-70 years old and 6 records for passengers from 70-80 years old. A second limitation is that 20% (177 records) of passenger records were missing age information.

6. Did survival between passengers from different ports of embarkation vary?¶

The differences in survival between passengers originating from different ports of embarkation was analyzed. The passengers originated from the ports of Cherbourg (C), Queenstown (Q), Southampton (S). The graphs below show the percentage of passengers who survived for each port and the survival number and total number of passengers.

Passengers originating from Cherbourg had the highest survival rate of 55%, while the passengers originating from Queenstown and Southampton had lower survival rates of 39 and 34% respectively. The majority of passengers (72%) originated from Southampton.

7. Conclusions¶

Several factors were correlated with differences in survival rates for passengers. 74% of female passengers survived while 19% of male passengers survived. In general, passengers in third class survived at a lower rate, with male passengers with a lower survival rate compared to female passengers across passengers classes. In addition, passengers less than 10 years old had the highest survival rate of 61%, with passengers from 10-70 years with survival rates from 35-45%. Passengers from the port of Cherbourg had a survival rate of 55% while passengers from Queenstown and Southampton had survival rates of 39 and 34%.

8. Limitations¶

A limitation of this analysis is that 20% or 177 records were missing age information. In addition, there were less records for passengers aged 60-80 years old.

This analysis explores correlation between factors and does not imply causation.
